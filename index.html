About

I am an assistant professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Middlebury College. Previously, I taught in the Concourse Program at MIT and was a postdoc at UGA. I received my PhD in mathematics from Tufts University, where I was advised by Robert Lemke Oliver, in 2022. Broadly speaking, my interests include number theory and arithmetic statistics.

Research

I am mainly interested in number theory and arithmetic statistics, particularly questions related to elliptic curves, Selmer groups, and counting number fields. I am also interested in prime number races, special values of L-functions, effective estimates, and some related topics in combinatorics.

Teaching

As a starting point for how I think about how to engage with the mathematical community, particularly as an educator, I believe in Federico Ardila's axioms:

  • Axiom 1: Mathematical potential is equally present in different groups, irrespective of geographic, demographic, and economic boundaries.
  • Axiom 2: Everyone can have joyful, meaningful, and empowering mathematical experiences.
  • Axiom 3: Mathematics is a powerful, malleable tool that can be shaped and used differently by various communities to serve their needs.
  • Axiom 4: Every student deserves to be treated with dignity and respect.
